Report

Imagine seeing chopsticks for the first time. Would you know its purpose and usage method? Affordances, real or perceived, are influenced by the user’s culture and knowledge and I must be sensitive to them.

How would government design everyday things? How would they assert their power and influence? How would society respond and react to symbolisms? Those are some of the questions I have when designing the Eastern Seaboard’s currency, travel documents and propaganda posters. Inspired by Affordance Theory and Design Thinking, I demonstrate the methods to design the artifacts for the Eastern Seaboard government, and why any designer should use Affordance Theory to design everyday things.

Read the report on how I applied Affordance Theory to get a solid A for this project.
